Reasons to visit Norway

1. Mountains

2. Fjords

3. Northen Lights

4. Culture

5. Midnight Sun




Norway is a country of stunning natural beauty, with fjords, mountains, and glaciers that will take your breath away. The Northern Lights can be seen in the winter and the Midnight Sun in the summer, making for unique and unforgettable experiences. The country is also known for its rich history and culture, with Viking ships, stave churches, and traditional folk music. Outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and fishing are popular and easily accessible, and the cities of Oslo and Bergen offer a mix of modern and traditional architecture and a thriving arts and food scene. Overall, Norway is a destination that offers something for everyone and is a must-visit for those who love nature and culture.


People began settling in Norway very early - in 9000 BC, in fact. This was after the end of the first ice age, so it was now an inhabitable location. Settlers survived by hunting and fishing animals such as seal, deer, elk and whales. Farming didn't become prominent until 500 BC, which was known as the bronze age. This time period got its name from the various weapons and jewelry created.


Oslo

Oslo is the economic and governmental centre of Norway. The city is also a hub of Norwegian trade, banking, industry and shipping.
